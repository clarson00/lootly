## TOKEN STRUCTURE

### $DOUBLOON Token

**What it is:** A utility token representing value in the RewardsPirate ecosystem.

**Backing:**
- Treasury reserves (USDC, yield-bearing stablecoins)
- Network subscription revenue
- Transaction fees
- Staking deposits

**Properties:**
- Fungible (1 doubloon = 1 doubloon everywhere)
- Transferable (between users, businesses)
- Redeemable (for goods, services, or crypto/fiat)
- Stakeable (for yield + governance)

**How they earn:**
- Check-ins and purchases at network businesses

**How they spend:**

### Price Stability

**Option A: Pegged to USD**
- 1 doubloon = $0.01 (or similar fixed rate)
- Easier for customers to understand
- Requires sufficient reserves to maintain peg
- Less speculative, more utility-focused

**Option B: Floating**
- Market determines price
- Could increase in value (rewards early adopters)
- More complex, more speculative
- Regulatory implications

**Recommendation:** Start with soft peg to USD, allow floating later as ecosystem matures.

### Key Questions

   - Utility token argument: Used for goods/services
   - Howey test considerations
   - Need legal counsel

2. **Money transmitter license?**
   - If converting doubloons â†” fiat
   - State-by-state requirements
   - May need MSB registration

3. **GENIUS Act implications**
   - Yield-bearing stablecoins restricted for US retail
   - Our structure: We hold assets, distribute "rewards"
   - May be compliant, needs legal review

4. **Tax implications**
   - Customer receiving yield = taxable?
   - Business staking = capital gains?
   - Need tax counsel

### Risk Mitigation

- Start with utility-only (no yield, no staking)
- Add features incrementally with legal review
- Consider offshore entity for token issuance
- Maintain clear separation: platform (US) vs token (TBD)
- Extensive disclosures and terms of service
