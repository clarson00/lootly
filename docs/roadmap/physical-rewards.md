## The VIP Card Deep Dive

### Why This Matters

A metal VIP card is:
1. **Premium feel** - Heavy, substantial, not plastic garbage
2. **Personalized** - Their name, their number, their QR
3. **Functional** - Actually works for check-ins
4. **Collectible** - People don't throw these away
5. **Conversation piece** - "What's that card?"

### Specs

```
Material: Stainless steel (matte black or brushed silver)
Size: Standard credit card (3.375" x 2.125")
Thickness: 0.8mm
Finish: Laser engraved

Front:
- Business logo (top)
- Customer name (middle)
- "VIP Member" or tier name (bottom)
- RewardsPirate small logo (corner)

Back:
- QR code (links to customer profile for check-in)
- Unique member ID number
- "Scan to check in"
- Business contact info (optional)
```

### Fulfillment Options

**Option A: RewardsPirate Handles It**
- We partner with metal card manufacturer
- Business orders through dashboard
- We ship to business or direct to customer
- Markup = revenue stream

**Option B: Business Handles It**
- We provide design templates
- Business uses their own vendor
- Lower cost, more control for them

**Option C: Hybrid**
- RewardsPirate preferred vendor (volume discount)
- Businesses can opt out and DIY

## Physical Rewards + Check-In Model

### The Action-Based Loop

```
Customer walks in
    ‚Üì
Checks in (phone number or QR scan)
    ‚Üì
Sees progress toward physical reward
    ‚Üì
"Only 50 more doubloons until my VIP card!"
    ‚Üì

    ‚Üì

    ‚Üì

```

### Why Check-Ins > Automatic Tracking

| Automatic (Square/Toast) | Check-In (RewardsPirate) |
|--------------------------|-------------------------|
| Passive | Active |
| Customer doesn't notice | Customer takes action |
| No anticipation | Sees progress each time |
| "Oh I have points?" | "I'm 50 away from my VIP card!" |
| No emotional investment | Working toward something tangible |

**The check-in IS the engagement.** It's not friction, it's ritual.
